Maria’s Learning Plan

What is your long term goal and or career pathway?

In terms of career pathway I’d like to expand from being a graphic designer to a digital designer with knowledge of web and other areas or maybe I’d like to work full time as a web developer for a few years. I’m not really fussed on job titles, I’m more wanting to find diverse and creative workspaces and employers who encourage growth. In 10 years time I’d love to be an Art/Creative Director.

What skills (non-technical - human skills) would you like to see developed in yourself while at EDA?

Skills I’d like to develop while I study would be my communication: I’d like to be more confident in presenting my ideas and/or starting discussions with my cohort. Problem solving and creativity are also skills I’d love to develop during my time as a student.

A description of your own strengths and limitations

My strengths are tolerant, reliable, charisma, altruism and natural leader which are traits that make me a ‘people-person’. I like being around people, helping them and accomplishing things together. My limitations are too idealistic/selfless/sensitive, fluctuating self-esteem and struggle to make tough decisions which I think are downfalls from being around people all the time. If I become too focused on other people’s struggles or voices, I run the risk of neglecting my own struggles and/or voice so it pays for me to take ‘breathers’ and check in with myself. Help myself before I help others kind-of-thing.

Based on the above description, a commitment to how you will manage your workload in this programme.

My strengths will enable me to be a good team player and I think I should be able to get along with the majority of people. I will make an effort to not get distracted by people or be a distraction myself and I will keep this in mind. Ways to do this could be limiting the amount of times I go on Slack(or my phone) or having a set time to check messages(like checking first thing after writing half of my 4 questions and once again after lunch break) and just by focusing on the work.

My limitations (too idealistic/selfless/sensitive, fluctuating self-esteem and struggle to make tough decisions) tie into the last point of not being distracted or being the distraction itself. I want to be more open to criticism and take it with a grain of salt, which I think I’m more than ready to try. I also want to share my own opinions more which has been a past struggle as I feel it makes me vulnerable but at the same time it’s better for people to know what I truly think or believe in - honesty is one of my close-guarded values after all! Ways to do the last point is by asking my cohort and facilitator for more or specific feedback e.g. instead of asking “Let me know what you think” I could ask someone for their view(politely) on something specific: “Hey @person I really like your use of padding and margins on your index page, what are ways I could improve mine?”

A commitment as to how and where you will seek help in a timely way.

The technical side of this course, I’ve just realised, will be the weaker side so when I get stuck my first port of call is google. I just have to really understand the problem first so that I can ask the right questions to get a solution. If I fail that way after a few attempts, then I will ask on Slack on the #foundations channel although I know there is a #code-help-desk channel as well. Usually at least one person will reply. If for some reason I am still stuck and I’m falling behind time I will try and see if I can change the work(usually its css code not working the way I want it to) and find an alternative way to achieve the task.

A description of what you expect from the facilitation team

To be honest, I don’t think I expect as much as I would from another tertiary school. The fact that we’re working from home and that the facilitators also have very busy schedules alongside teaching bootcamp students tells me I shouldn’t rely on these guys too much during the foundation weeks. I think this mentality will change when I start the bootcamp in October. Although, if I was having serious issues with managing workload, harassment from a fellow student etc then I would definitely contact Emily!

Any scheduling information such as block-out times when you are committed to other things.

Scheduling information: So I wake up early, get the kids off to school at 8.30am every morning. I get back home between 8.40am-9.00am except on Tuesdays where I go do the grocery shopping and then get back home about 10am. Friday after-school, I have my two oldest kids stay at their dad’s until Sunday noon, so I will use this window to catch up with my studies.
